About

00

I research augmented and virtual reality systems that connect physical spaces, artworks, and people through ubiquitous computing.

I am an M.S. student in the UVR Lab at KAIST, advised by Prof. Woontack Woo. My work spans AR exhibition and spatial experience, augmenting architectural elements from 2D artworks, and studying user experience and VR comfort through behavioral analysis.

Projects

Research
WISE UI Ongoing 2025 – Present
KAIST UVR Lab · Augmented Space-Time team
  • A platform that brings a shared UI to everyone using AR glasses — reconstructing the surrounding space and the user’s location at a consistent level in any environment, estimating integrated attention from physical cues together with the user’s personal traits and nearby objects, and enabling organic, personalized information augmentation and interaction across diverse spaces.
  • My role: on-device (HMD) recognition and tracking of everyday objects around the user — building an efficient system that lightens tracking by leveraging the user’s actions (grasp / gaze).
Coursework
K Demon Stress Hunters Course 2025 Fall
MV6000 · Augmented Reality Project · KAIST
  • A K-Culture-based AR system for implicit stress relief through play. A three-stage embodied interaction (Affect Labeling → Externalization → Relational Healing) lets users objectify negative emotions and resolve them through playful manipulation, drawing on Korean motifs (Moon Jar, folk paintings, Jeong). A pilot study showed increased positive affect and reduced state anxiety and stress. (First author)
  • Demo video ↗ (Korean)
3D Interaction Design — “Short-cut” Course 2025
GCT555 · 3D Interaction Design · KAIST
  • 2D shortcuts like Ctrl+C / V are universal, but 3D interfaces have no shared gesture vocabulary yet. Short-cut introduces a familiar, intuitive 3D shortcut system that maps Ctrl+C / F / Z / X directly to ASL finger-spelling gestures.
  • A camera tracks both hands in real time and recognizes Copy / Find / Undo / Delete (distinguishing left and right hands); users then select and place blocks in 3D space with a pinch interaction, with real-time visual feedback on match status.
