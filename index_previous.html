AR/VR/MR · Computer Vision · HCI

Building intelligent AR systems that understand and adapt to the real world.

I research how AR/VR systems can perceive, reason about, and interact with dynamic real-world environments in real time.

I am a Ph.D. student at KAIST GSCT, working on Augmented Reality and Mixed Reality systems with a focus on real-time scene understanding and 3D interaction. My current research centers on a Dynamic Functional Scene Graph for AR glasses that leverages HOI-driven event inference and LLM-based affordance reasoning to maintain on-device state consistency.

I also work on collaborative AR guidance interfaces for assembly tasks — designing expert-beginner interaction systems informed by principles of intuitiveness, cost-effectiveness, and user controllability. My broader interests span egocentric video understanding, K-culture inspired immersive computing, and physiological-social presence in VR.

Research Projects

Dynamic Functional Scene Graph for AR Glasses Ongoing
20XX – Present | KAIST
  • Designing a real-time scene graph architecture for AR glasses that maintains on-device state consistency through HOI-driven event inference.
  • Leveraging LLM-based condition evaluation and affordance reasoning over a Node/Edge JSON structure to enable proactive context-aware AR overlays.
  • Implemented a server-client split architecture (Unity/Python, WebSocket) supporting multiple simultaneous camera streams for edge-cloud processing.
  • Adopted V-JEPA 2 for interaction detection and LLM for condition/affordance inference as key design decisions for the inference pipeline.
Beginner–Expert Collaborative AR Assembly Guidance Interface Ongoing
20XX – Present | KAIST
  • Designing an AR interface to bridge skill gaps between novice and expert users during physical assembly tasks, using a split LED wall layout for shared awareness.
  • Developed multi-state feedback design informed by expert interviews to support real-time guidance without cognitive overload.
  • Interface architecture grounded in 3D interaction principles: intuitiveness, cost-effectiveness, and user controllability.
K-Culture Inspired AR Wellness Application
20XX – 20XX | KAIST
